Collab Manager Project

This project is a full stack web application deployed to administer collab hours in CSCI0320: Introduction to Software Engineering at Brown.

Process

I developed this project on a team of 4 for my final project as a student in CSCI0320. Our idea came from the lack of organization of collab hours in the course, where students in the course serve as one anothers' TAs. Prior to our system, the real TAs used google forms to pair students serving as TAs and students needing help. We realized we could streamline this process in one full stack application.

We built the backend in Java and the frontend with React and TypeScript. In particular, I focused on building the backend and ensuring that it was thread safe and could handle requests from many students at once. I did so through two queues, one from students serving as TAs and one for students needing help, and continuously popped and paired from the queues.

Result

Two semesters after developing this application as my final project in CSCI0320, I became a TA for the course. I pitched the idea of using my application in practice, and the head TAs agreed. This gave me the unique opportunity to deploy the frontend and backend and test the application with real users.

After I deployed the system for the course, it experienced a variety of concurrency and storage issues, which I resolved throughout the semester. The application is still currently live and available to CSCI0320 students below.
